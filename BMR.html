What is BMR?

About Basal Metabolic Rate

BMR stands for Basal Metabolic Rate and represents the amount of calories the human body needs to accomplish its most basic life-sustaining functions.

Basal Metabolic Rate characterises the number of calories neccessary for the human body to function properly, since it is always engaged in breathing, circulation, nutrient processing and cell production.

Did you know that your body burns calories even in your sleep?

It is not a small number of them. According to a Harvard publication, listing the number of calories burnt in different activities or in daily life circumstances, a person weighing 125 pounds (approximately 57 kilograms) is burning 38 calories in just an hour of sleep. Amplify this with the recommended 8 hours of sleep and take into consideration that the number is increasing proportionately with the weight.

What is RMR?

RMR (Resting Metabolic Rate) is often confused with BMR as their values are so close to each other. While BMR is a minimum number of calories required for basic functions at rest, RMR — also called resting energy expenditure (REE) — is the number of calories that your body burns while it’s at rest.

Why should I know my BMR?

People take advantage in finding out their BMRs due to its help in specifing the personalised calorie intake for weight loss, gain or for maintaining the current weight. The principle is simple:

  • Do you want to maintain your weight? Eat accordingly to your body needs. Consume as much as you burn.
  • Do you want to lose weight? Eat less calories than you burn.
  • Do you want to gain weight? Eat more calories than you burn. That way you will be in a calorie surplus.
